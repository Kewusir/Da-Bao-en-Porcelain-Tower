1412 to 1431

Yongle’s Imperial Reconstruction

Under the Yongle Emperor, the Great Bao'en Temple was rebuilt at monumental scale in Nanjing and crowned by the celebrated nine-story glazed pagoda.

Historical Significance

This was the defining imperial phase of Bao'en Temple. It linked Buddhist devotion, dynastic authority, and the ceremonial importance of Nanjing into one powerful architectural statement.

Spatial and Architectural Reading

The complex culminated in a tall glazed pagoda whose luminous skin distinguished it from ordinary masonry towers. The building embodied technical refinement and political visibility at once.

Memory and Legacy

Most later accounts of Bao'en Temple take this Ming phase as the monument’s canonical form. It remains the reference point for nearly every modern reconstruction of the site’s image.
