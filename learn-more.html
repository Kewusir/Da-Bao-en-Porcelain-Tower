Historical Layers

The site is best understood as a layered religious landscape rather than a single monument. Song-period remains, the Ming imperial temple, later destruction, and modern museum interpretation all coexist within one narrative.

Spatial Reading

The stricture of the temple can be framed around three spatial ideas: the vertical monument of the pagoda, the buried continuity of the underground palace, and the museum as a curated interface between archaeology and public memory. (Find out more inside the temple)
