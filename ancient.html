1011 and 2008

Song Crypt Beneath the Ming Temple

An archaeological reading of the hidden Song layer beneath the later Ming monument.

Overview

Excavations in 2008 revealed the underground palace of the earlier Changgan Temple, including a Northern Song crypt dated to 1011 and an important reliquary assemblage.

Historical Significance

This discovery pushed the story of Bao'en Temple beneath the visible Ming monument and re-established the site as a layered sacred landscape with deeper historical continuity.

Spatial and Architectural Reading

The underground palace reveals a hidden ritual order below later rebuilding. It shows how later dynastic architecture was built over, and in dialogue with, older sacred foundations rather than on a neutral site.

Memory and Legacy

The archaeological discoveries gave modern Bao'en Temple its strongest material anchor and transformed the site from a story of loss alone into one of historical depth and continuity.
